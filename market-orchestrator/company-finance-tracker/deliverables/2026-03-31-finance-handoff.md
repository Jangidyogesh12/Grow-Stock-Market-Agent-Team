# Finance Handoff

## Report Date

2026-03-31

## Companies Reviewed

- GAIL
- PTC India
- IOC
- Ashok Leyland
- Adani Power

## Company Analysis

### GAIL

- Business snapshot: State-owned gas transmission, trading, LPG, and petrochemicals platform with strategic pipeline infrastructure and utility-like positioning in India's gas ecosystem.
- Growth and profitability: Revenue CAGR is about 15% over three years, but profit growth has been softer recently; FY25 sales were Rs 141,903 crore and net profit was Rs 12,463 crore, with ROE near 13% and ROCE near 14%.
- Balance sheet or leverage: Borrowings rose to about Rs 21,595 crore in FY25, but reserves of Rs 78,422 crore and book value near the share price keep the balance sheet manageable.
- Cash flow: Operating cash flow rebounded to Rs 15,735 crore in FY25 and free cash flow improved to about Rs 7,818 crore after a weak FY23-FY24 stretch.
- Valuation: Around 10.5x earnings, about 1.0x book, and roughly 5.45% dividend yield; valuation looks reasonable for a large PSU energy franchise.
- Risks: Gas marketing and petrochemical earnings remain commodity-sensitive, returns are not especially high, and ongoing capex can keep leverage elevated.
- Finance verdict: Best mix here of valuation support, dividend income, strategic asset base, and improving cash generation; financially the cleanest fresh-review name in this set.

### PTC India

- Business snapshot: Power trader with cross-border relevance and exchange exposure, but still fundamentally a lower-margin intermediary business.
- Growth and profitability: Sales have been flat to declining over longer periods, though profit improved in FY25; FY25 revenue was Rs 16,241 crore and net profit was Rs 976 crore, but part of earnings strength came from high other income.
- Balance sheet or leverage: Debt has reduced sharply from Rs 6,546 crore in FY23 to about Rs 2,962 crore in FY25, which is a meaningful positive.
- Cash flow: Cash conversion is strong on recent numbers, with FY25 operating cash flow around Rs 2,132 crore and free cash flow around Rs 2,127 crore.
- Valuation: About 7.5x earnings, 0.8x book, and roughly 7.43% dividend yield; headline valuation is cheap.
- Risks: Low promoter holding, weak sales growth, modest returns, and earnings quality is less clean because other income is large relative to reported profit.
- Finance verdict: Attractive as a value-and-yield idea, but lower quality than GAIL because the business is slower-growing and part of the profit story is non-core.

### IOC

- Business snapshot: India's largest integrated downstream oil player with refining, marketing, pipeline, gas, and petrochemical exposure, giving it major scale but also cyclical earnings.
- Growth and profitability: FY24 was exceptionally strong, but FY25 normalized sharply; FY25 revenue was Rs 758,106 crore and net profit was Rs 13,789 crore versus Rs 43,161 crore in FY24, with ROE down to about 6.5% and ROCE about 7.4%.
- Balance sheet or leverage: Borrowings remain very large at roughly Rs 152,271 crore in FY25, though this is partly a function of scale and working-capital intensity across the refining/marketing model.
- Cash flow: Operating cash flow stayed positive at about Rs 34,699 crore in FY25, but free cash flow was only about Rs 421 crore because capex remains heavy.
- Valuation: Around 5.35x earnings, near 1.0x book, and about 5.17% dividend yield; cheap multiples reflect cyclicality and margin uncertainty rather than obvious mispricing.
- Risks: Refining margins can swing fast, crude volatility and policy intervention can compress profitability, and leverage plus large capex reduces financial flexibility.
- Finance verdict: Cheap and strategically important, but not a clean balance-sheet compounding story; better framed as a cyclical, policy-sensitive hold/watch than a high-conviction fresh buy.

### Ashok Leyland

- Business snapshot: Major commercial vehicle manufacturer with strong domestic M&HCV positioning and improving scale, but still exposed to auto-cycle, rates, fuel costs, and freight demand.
- Growth and profitability: Operating performance has improved materially; FY25 revenue was Rs 48,535 crore and net profit was Rs 3,383 crore, with TTM revenue around Rs 53,811 crore and ROE near 28.8%.
- Balance sheet or leverage: Borrowings climbed to about Rs 49,962 crore in FY25 and remain high relative to net worth; promoter pledge is also a balance-sheet/governance overhang.
- Cash flow: Cash generation is the weak point: FY25 operating cash flow was only Rs 128 crore and free cash flow stayed negative at roughly Rs 1,471 crore despite strong reported earnings.
- Valuation: About 25.1x earnings and 7.2x book; the stock already discounts a lot of the operating recovery.
- Risks: High leverage, weak cash conversion, pledged promoter stake, and sensitivity to CV demand and input/funding costs.
- Finance verdict: Good operating momentum but the weakest financial risk-reward in this set because leverage and cash-flow quality lag the earnings narrative.

### Adani Power

- Business snapshot: India's largest private thermal power producer with a fleet tied to long-term PPAs, merchant sales, and integrated coal/logistics support across the Adani ecosystem.
- Growth and profitability: FY25 revenue rose to about Rs 56,203 crore from Rs 50,351 crore, while net profit normalized to roughly Rs 12,750 crore from an unusually strong Rs 20,829 crore in FY24; even after that reset, ROE stayed near 26.1% and ROCE near 22.5%.
- Balance sheet or leverage: Borrowings were about Rs 39,495 crore in FY25 versus Rs 34,862 crore in FY24, so leverage is still material, but the company also carries a much stronger reserve base of roughly Rs 52,490 crore than in prior years.
- Cash flow: Cash generation is a major strength; FY25 operating cash flow was about Rs 21,501 crore and free cash flow was about Rs 9,957 crore even after a heavy investment year.
- Valuation: Around 25.3x earnings and roughly 5x book at the latest market price, with no dividend yield; this is no longer a cheap utility multiple despite strong returns.
- Risks: Earnings still depend on thermal demand, coal economics, regulation, and merchant tariff conditions; expansion activity and still-large borrowings can raise execution and leverage risk, and the lack of dividend reduces valuation support.
- Finance verdict: Fundamentally strong with excellent cash generation and returns, but financially it is a higher-beta, less valuation-supported idea than GAIL because the business is more cyclical and the stock already discounts a lot of strength.

## Cross-Company Takeaways

- Strongest financial profile: GAIL still has the best all-around finance setup for a fresh call because it combines manageable leverage, recovering cash flow, low valuation, and dividend support; Adani Power is stronger operationally, but much less forgiving on valuation.
- Weakest financial profile: Ashok Leyland, because leverage is high, free cash flow remains weak, and promoter pledge adds an extra balance-sheet/governance concern.
- Important comparison note: Adani Power now stands out as the strongest profit-and-cash-flow engine in the group, but GAIL remains the cleaner finance risk-reward because Adani Power's thermal exposure and richer valuation leave less room for error.

## Key Notes For Orchestrator

- GAIL is the best finance-backed fresh-review candidate; it is not a high-growth story, but valuation, dividend, and FY25 cash-flow recovery make the risk-reward relatively steady.
- Adani Power brings the strongest current operating and cash-flow profile in this basket, but the orchestrator should frame it as quality-at-a-price rather than a cheap defensive utility because valuation is much richer and thermal/regulatory risk remains real.
- PTC India is a cheaper yield play than GAIL, but the orchestrator should flag that a meaningful part of recent earnings strength came from other income, so quality is lower.
- IOC should be framed as cheap-for-a-reason: FY25 profit normalized hard from FY24, debt is large, and heavy capex keeps free cash flow thin.
- Ashok Leyland's operating turnaround is real, but weak cash conversion, high borrowings, and promoter pledge make it the most fragile finance profile among the four names.
